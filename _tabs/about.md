I like to fix things. Broken things are a puzzle and I like to solve puzzles. Some of that is 
just how my brain works. A good measure of it comes from my father. He was the most capable person
I've ever known. He taught me enough lessons to fill a tool chest. Lessons about how to do things right; 
and what to do when you don't. When inspiration and memories intersect, I've written a few of those stories here.

[8N1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asynchronous_serial_communication), or eightnoneone, was the earliest bit of computer _incantation_ I could remember learning. The dawn of personal computers happened when I was an early teenager. 
In that era, getting any two devices to communicate was often a matter of configuring RS-232 serial connections. Understanding things like baud rates, parity, and stop bits was completely new knowledge in my world. In my small town, mastering it felt like a superpower.

For me and my career, `8N1` was the beginning.
